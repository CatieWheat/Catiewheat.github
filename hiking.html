Why I Hike

There's something about being out on a trail, surrounded by trees, mountains, and fresh air. It resets everything. Hiking has taught me patience, perseverance, and how to appreciate the small things. Whether it's a quick 2-mile loop or a full-day summit push, every hike leaves me feeling better than when I started.

Essential Gear

Good gear makes all the difference. Here's what I never hit the trail without, organized by priority:

  1. Safety Essentials
    • Map and compass (don't rely only on your phone)
    • First aid kit
    • Emergency whistle
    • Rain jacket or emergency poncho
  2. Navigation & Comfort
    • Trekking poles
    • Headlamp with extra batteries
    • Sunscreen and sunglasses
  3. Food & Water
    • At least 2 liters of water per person
    • High-energy snacks (trail mix, bars, jerky)
    • Water filter or purification tablets
  4. Clothing & Footwear
    • Moisture-wicking base layers
    • Sturdy, broken-in hiking boots
    • Wool or synthetic hiking socks
